By Federico Barrera Roccatti

Joe Burrow has already established himself as one of the NFL’s elite quarterbacks, the clear face of the Cincinnati Bengals franchise. Drafted No. 1 overall in 2020 after leading LSU to a national championship and winning the Heisman Trophy, Burrow brought with him the reputation of a poised, accurate, and fearless leader. His rookie season was cut short by a devastating ACL injury, but his comeback showed the resilience that defines him. In just his second season, he led the Bengals to the Super Bowl, combining sharp decision-making with an aggressive downfield mindset that rejuvenated Cincinnati’s offense. Since then, Burrow has built a reputation for toughness, calm under pressure, and surgical accuracy. He’s developed one of the NFL’s most feared passing attacks with Ja’Marr Chase and Tee Higgins, showing the ability to shred defenses both in rhythm and on broken plays. Injuries have slowed him at times — particularly in 2023 with a calf strain that limited his early-season explosiveness — but when healthy, Burrow is as complete a quarterback as there is in the game. What makes Burrow special isn’t just stats or highlight throws, it’s the way he carries himself. He exudes confidence, sets the tone for the locker room, and has become one of the NFL’s most respected leaders at just 27 years old. With his accuracy, pocket presence, and competitive edge, Burrow has the Bengals firmly in the mix as perennial contenders, and he’s already carved out a place as one of the defining quarterbacks of his era.
