By Federico Barrera Roccatti

Josh Allen is the definition of a modern NFL quarterback — physically overwhelming, wildly competitive, and capable of taking over games with either his arm or his legs. Drafted 7th overall in 2018 by the Buffalo Bills, Allen came into the league as a raw prospect from Wyoming with big tools and bigger questions about his accuracy. Over the years, he has not only silenced those doubts but has emerged as one of the most feared signal-callers in football. Standing at 6’5” and 237 pounds, Allen blends the power of a linebacker with the arm strength to hit any throw on the field. He’s piled up multiple 4,000-yard passing seasons, added double-digit rushing touchdown campaigns, and has consistently been among the league leaders in total touchdowns. What sets him apart is the chaos he thrives in — escaping pressure, hurdling defenders, and ripping 40-yard lasers off balance. His physicality gives Buffalo an offense that’s never out of a game, no matter the deficit. At the same time, Allen’s gunslinger mentality cuts both ways. His turnovers and risky throws remain part of his story, but it’s also what makes him so dangerous — he’s never afraid to take the shot that breaks a defense. Since 2020, he has turned Buffalo into a perennial contender, leading the Bills to multiple AFC East titles and deep playoff runs. Though the Super Bowl has eluded him so far, Allen has already proven he belongs in the elite tier of quarterbacks, a player whose presence alone makes Buffalo a championship threat.
